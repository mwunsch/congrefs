In Congress

The script follows the Rules for Long S:

  • A short S is used at the end of words (e.g. his, complains, success).
  • A short S is used before an apostrophe (e.g. clos'd, us'd).
  • A short S is used before the letter 'F' (e.g. satisfaction, misfortune, transfute, transfix, transfer, successful).
  • A long S is used initially and medially except for the exceptions noted above (e.g. song, use, press, substitute).
  • A short S is used before a hyphen in compound words with the first element ending in the letter 'S' (e.g. cross-piece, cross-examination, Press-work, bird's-nest).
